Shree Ramanujacharya Evam Vishistadvaita Darshan

The present book Shree Ramanujacharya Evam Vishistadvaita Darshan, that roughly translates as Shree Ramanujacharya and the Vishistadvaiata Philosophy written by Dr. Raghunath and other scholars deals with the nature and character of the personality of the Vaishnavite saint Ramanuja and the philosophy he propounded, namely Vishistadvaita Vedanta. The book is an abridged version of the philosophy presented in a simplified manner in Nepali for the benefit of common readers who may not be well-versed in Sanskrit. The philosopher, born approximately 1000 years ago in the south Indian city of Sriperembudur, also known as Bhutpuri expounded and propagated, under the broad realm of the Vedic philosophy, the path of devotion (bhakti) and unconditional surrender (prapatti) as essential tenets to attainment of Mokshya. Having lived a long life of 120 years, the philosopher saint vanquished all non-Vedic philosophies then en vogue, established temples and places of worship far and wide and initiated modes of worship consentaneous to authoritative vedic texts relevant on the subject.
